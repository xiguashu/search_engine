 'tis sin  flatter; 'good'     better:

    preposterous; therefore,  'good lord.'

 gloucester  thy  son  kill'd   presumption. 

 king henry vi  hadst  thou  kill'd   thou didst presume, 
 thou  hadst  lived  kill a son  mine. 
     prophesy,   a thousand, 
    mistrust  parcel   fear, 
      man's sigh   a  widow  's, 
     orphan's water-standing eye-- 
 men    sons, wives   husbands, 
   orphans   parents  timeless  death-- 
   rue  hour   thou wast born. 
   owl shriek'd  thy birth,--an evil  sign  ; 
   night-crow cried, aboding luckless time; 
 dogs  howl'd,  hideous tempest shook  trees; 
   raven  rook  'd    chimney's top, 
   chattering pies  dismal discords sung. 
 thy  mother felt   a mother's pain,

   wit  ,  indigested  deformed lump, 
     fruit   a goodly tree.
